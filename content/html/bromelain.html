Bromelain (Ananas comosus)

Systemic enzyme therapy backed by science. A proteolytic enzyme derived from the pineapple plant, bromelain supports protein digestion, modulates inflammation, and enhances recovery — a powerful, natural adjunct for joint, muscle and digestive wellness.

What is Bromelain?

Bromelain is a proteolytic enzyme complex extracted from the stem and fruit of the pineapple plant, Ananas comosus — a species native to South America and now cultivated widely across tropical regions, including India. Chemically, it is a thiol endopeptidase: a cysteine protease enzyme complex that breaks down protein and exerts systemic anti-inflammatory effects far beyond the digestive tract.

In traditional medicine systems, pineapple has long been used to enhance digestion, support vitality and accelerate healing. Bromelain's enzymatic activity and systemic anti-inflammatory effects have elevated its use in modern integrative therapies, where it is valued as a natural adjunct for post-surgical recovery, sports performance and digestive wellness.

From a humble tropical fruit to a therapeutic enzyme, bromelain bridges traditional and modern medicine. Pineapple has been used across tropical cultures to aid digestion and wound healing, and these uses have since been substantiated by a growing body of pharmacological research.

  • Extraction method — aqueous extraction followed by ultrafiltration or freeze-drying
  • Source material — derived from the stem and fruit of Ananas comosus

Clinically Studied Benefits

Bromelain has been studied across a range of inflammatory, post-surgical and recovery applications. The mechanisms below are drawn from the published pharmacological literature.

Anti-inflammatory activity

  • Inhibits NF-κB activation and downregulates iNOS and COX-2 expression
  • Suppresses MAPK signalling (ERK, JNK, p38), reducing TNF-α, IL-6 and nitric oxide
  • Supports inflammation resolution without the GI side effects seen with NSAIDs

Pain relief & edema reduction

  • Reduces post-surgical pain, swelling and bruising
  • Decreases inflammatory mediator release and modulates prostaglandin pathways
  • Effective in oral surgery, sports injuries and sinusitis

Muscle recovery & exercise performance

  • Minimizes exercise-induced muscle damage markers (CK, LDH)
  • Helps maintain testosterone levels during intense training
  • Enhances recovery across multiple-day athletic events

Saliva enhancement & respiratory support

  • Bromelain–glucosylceramide improves mucosal hydration and skin barrier function, useful in dry-mouth and radiation-induced xerostomia
  • Mucolytic and anti-inflammatory action in the respiratory tract
  • Reduces cough frequency and upper-respiratory irritation
