Clinical questions, answered

Frequently asked by healthcare professionals.

Are your extracts standardized for active compounds?

Yes. Each extract is standardized for bioactives — for example, Bacopa monnieri for bacosides A & B, Boswellia for AKBA, and Amla for emblicanin A & B — to ensure batch-to-batch reproducibility.

What quality and safety standards do you follow?

Greenspace Herbs follows FSSAI, WHO-GMP, and clean-label standards. Extracts are free from heavy metals, solvents, aflatoxins, and pesticide residues.

Which conditions do your actives support?

Our portfolio aligns with clinical endpoints such as inflammation markers, glycemic control, lipid profiles, and hormonal panels. Representative actives include:

  • Ashwagandha — adaptogen for stress and cortisol modulation.
  • Guduchi — immunomodulator and hepatoprotective.
  • Fenugreek — glucose-lowering and hormonal balance.

Do you have actives for cognitive and stress support?

Bacopa monnieri, Centella asiatica, and Withania somnifera support memory, mental clarity, and stress resilience through neurotrophic and adaptogenic mechanisms.

Can these extracts be co-administered with conventional therapies?

Yes, under medical supervision. Most extracts are safe to co-administer with conventional therapies, especially in non-acute conditions like metabolic syndrome, anxiety, and perimenopausal symptoms.

Is there scientific documentation available for each ingredient?

Yes. Each monograph includes references to in vivo / in vitro studies, clinical trials, and pharmacodynamic mechanisms. Downloadable scientific briefs are available on request.

Do you offer custom or synergistic blends?

Greenspace Herbs offers proprietary blends using combinations such as Ashwagandha + Bacopa + Amla for adaptogenic and antioxidant synergy. Customization is possible for clinical partners.

How are your herbs sourced?

Herbs are ethically and ecologically sourced from Indian smallholder farms and cooperatives. Each batch undergoes origin verification and sustainability review.

How can I request clinical documentation?

Healthcare professionals can request Certificates of Analysis (CoAs), safety data sheets, and clinical summaries by contacting our medical liaison team or through our contact page.
