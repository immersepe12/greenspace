Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata)

A standardized extract of Boswellia serrata — Shallaki in Ayurveda — delivering clinically validated analgesic, anti-inflammatory and joint-health benefits where ancient relief meets modern research.

What is Boswellia serrata?

Boswellia serrata, known as Shallaki in Ayurveda, is a resin-producing tree native to India's arid regions. For centuries, its fragrant gum — Indian Frankincense — has been used to address inflammation, pain and joint conditions. Referenced in ancient Ayurvedic texts such as the Charaka Samhita, it is traditionally revered for promoting Sandhigata Vata (joint health) and managing chronic musculoskeletal disorders.

Today, that legacy of healing and mobility is matched by a growing body of clinical evidence: standardized boswellic-acid extracts are studied for osteoarthritis pain relief, anti-arthritic action and broader metabolic modulation.

Botanical profile

  • Botanical name: Boswellia serrata Roxb. ex Colebr.
  • Common names: Shallaki, Indian Frankincense, Salai Guggal
  • Habitat: Dry mountainous forests of India, North Africa and the Middle East

Where does it grow?

The tree thrives in dry, mountainous deciduous forests, tolerating poor, rocky soils and arid climates. In India it is harvested across the central and western states — notably Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh — with related ranges extending into North Africa and the Middle East.

Phytochemical signature

Key bioactives

The therapeutic activity of the resin is driven by pentacyclic triterpenic boswellic acids — most notably AKBA (3-O-acetyl-11-keto-β-boswellic acid) — alongside lupeolic acid, serratol, phellandrene and limonene.

Chemical classes

Triterpenes, sesquiterpenes and diterpenes form the principal constituent classes of the oleo-gum resin.

Standardization range

Extracts are standardized to defined levels of total boswellic acids and AKBA, with clinically validated formats such as Aflapin® and 5-Loxin® offering enriched AKBA content for enhanced potency.

Mechanism of action

Boswellic acids act primarily by inhibiting 5-lipoxygenase (5-LOX), reducing leukotriene-mediated inflammation, and by suppressing pro-inflammatory cytokines including TNF-α, IL-1β and IL-6. This dual action underpins the herb's analgesic and anti-arthritic profile.

Clinically studied benefits

Pain relief & joint function

Inhibition of 5-LOX alleviates joint pain, morning stiffness and swelling, improving mobility and joint function. (Majeed et al., Phytotherapy Research, 2019.)

Anti-arthritic action

Suppression of pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6) supports a measurable anti-arthritic effect. (Banji et al., Journal of Ethnopharmacology, 2022.)

Metabolic & cancer modulation

Emerging evidence indicates Boswellia may lower blood glucose and enhance insulin sensitivity, while inhibiting cancer-cell proliferation and angiogenesis (VEGF, MMP-9, PGE2). (Mehrzadi et al., J Evid Based Integr Med, 2018.)
