Coleus (Coleus forskohlii (Willd.) Briq.)

A clinically backed botanical for weight management, cardiac function and more — standardized to forskolin, the diterpenoid that activates cellular cyclic AMP (cAMP) pathways.

What is Coleus forskohlii?

Coleus forskohlii, commonly known as Gandira in Ayurveda, is valued for its rich content of forskolin — a key diterpenoid compound that activates cellular cyclic AMP (cAMP) pathways. Standardized extracts from this plant are widely used in modern health applications including metabolic health, cardiovascular support and body-composition management.

Origins & Ethnobotany

Coleus forskohlii is native to India and thrives in subtropical and tropical regions, especially in the lower Himalayan belt and southern Indian states such as Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. In traditional medicine systems it has been used to support digestion, promote heart function and address respiratory conditions such as asthma — a traditional remedy now meeting modern metabolic needs.

Bioactive Compounds

The activity of Coleus forskohlii is anchored in a distinctive profile of diterpenoids, with forskolin as the principal driver:

  • Forskolin — the principal diterpenoid responsible for cAMP activation
  • Barbatusin, coleon O — supporting diterpenoids
  • Ferruginol, sugiol, manoyl oxide — additional phytochemical constituents

Chemical class: Diterpenoids. Extracts are typically standardized to a defined forskolin content for consistent activity.

Clinically Studied Benefits

Weight Management

Forskolin stimulates lipolysis via activation of adenylate cyclase and increases intracellular cAMP levels, promoting fat metabolism.

Anti-Glaucoma Activity

It decreases intraocular pressure by enhancing aqueous-humor outflow, activating cAMP in the ciliary body to regulate fluid dynamics in the eye.

Cardioprotective Effects

Forskolin improves myocardial contractility and cardiac output, and reduces vascular resistance through enhanced cAMP signaling.

Anti-Vitiligo Activity

It stimulates melanocyte proliferation and melanogenesis, promoting repigmentation in vitiligo lesions through increased cAMP.

Ayurvedic Context

In classical practice Coleus forskohlii is directed at Kapha imbalance and metabolic stagnation. It features in synergistic herbal blends with Salacia reticulata and Sesamum indicum for metabolic regulation, and supports digestion, cardiac and respiratory function within traditional formulations.
