Moringa (Moringa oleifera Lam.)

Nutritional support and overall wellness from the "Drumstick Tree." Traditionally valued in Ayurveda and now supported by modern science, Moringa oleifera offers a wide range of health benefits — rich in alkaloids, flavonoids and phenolics.

What is Moringa oleifera?

Native to the Indian subcontinent, Moringa oleifera thrives in tropical and subtropical climates across India, Africa and Southeast Asia. Known as the "Drumstick Tree," it is cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves, pods, seeds and flowers. Traditionally used in Ayurveda to boost immunity, treat malnutrition, support blood sugar control and improve skin health, it has become one of the most studied nutritional botanicals of the modern era.

Origins & Ethnobotany

Native to the Indian subcontinent, Moringa oleifera thrives in tropical and subtropical climates across India, Africa and Southeast Asia. Known as the "Drumstick Tree," it is cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves, pods, seeds and flowers.

Traditionally used in Ayurveda to boost immunity, treat malnutrition, support blood sugar control and improve skin health, Moringa has long been regarded as a near-complete source of plant nutrition.

Key Bioactives

Moringa's broad activity profile is driven by a diverse range of flavonoids, phenolics and unique phytochemicals concentrated in its leaves and seeds:

  • Rutin, Kaempferol, Quercetin, Isoquercetin, Moringine — flavonoid and alkaloid constituents underlying antioxidant and metabolic activity
  • Niazimicin — associated with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activity
  • β-Carotene, Campesterol, Chlorogenic acid — supporting nutritional and metabolic function

Clinical Validation

Research demonstrates Moringa oleifera's role in lowering blood glucose, reducing blood pressure, modulating lipid profiles, enhancing antioxidant defenses and supporting skin health through collagen preservation.

Mechanism of Action

  • Enhances insulin secretion and sensitivity via flavonoid-mediated pathways
  • Lowers blood pressure through vasodilation and nitric oxide modulation
  • Inhibits elastase and collagenase to slow skin aging
  • Exhibits antimicrobial effects by disrupting microbial cell walls
