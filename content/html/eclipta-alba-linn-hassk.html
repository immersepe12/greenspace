Bhringaraja (Eclipta alba)

Widely known in Ayurveda as the "King of Hair," Eclipta alba is a time-honored Rasayana herb delivering clinically supported benefits for liver health and hair regeneration — a potent phytoactive for holistic wellness formulations.

What is Bhringaraja?

Rich in wedelolactone and ecliptin, Eclipta alba (Linn.) Hassk — known across Ayurveda as Bhringaraja, the "King of Hair" — is a herb renowned for its dual benefits for liver health and hair regeneration. It delivers hepatoprotective, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial and hair-revitalizing effects, making it a versatile active for modern nutraceutical and cosmetic formulations.

Origins & Ethnobotany

Native to India and tropical Asia, Eclipta alba has been a staple of Ayurvedic, Unani and traditional Siddha medicine systems. Used both orally and topically, it supports liver detoxification, helps prevent premature graying and restores vitality. Traditionally administered in oils, teas and tonics, it is classified as a Rasayana herb for rejuvenation and longevity.

Key Bioactives

  • Wedelolactone, Ecliptin, Luteolin, Apigenin, Demethylwedelolactone — the principal phytoactives driving its therapeutic profile
  • Belongs to the coumestan, flavonoid and triterpenoid classes
  • Standardized extracts typically contain ≥ 3% wedelolactone

Clinically Studied Benefits

Across published preclinical and clinical work, Eclipta alba demonstrates a consistent, multi-system pharmacological signature:

  • Supports liver regeneration and reduces liver enzyme levels (AST, ALT)
  • Promotes hair growth by prolonging the anagen (growth) phase and activating dermal papillae
  • Reduces inflammation by modulating TNF-α, IL-6 and iNOS pathways
  • Provides antimicrobial protection against pathogens like Staphylococcus aureus and Candida albicans
  • Exerts neuroprotective effects through antioxidant activity in neural tissue

Mechanism of Action

  • Hepatoprotective: scavenges free radicals, restores antioxidant enzymes and modulates liver enzyme expression
  • Hair revitalization: activates the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway in hair follicles
  • Anti-inflammatory: inhibits NF-κB and nitric oxide production
  • Neuroprotective: prevents oxidative damage in neurons by upregulating antioxidant enzymes

Ideal Formats

  • Herbal teas, tinctures, capsules and oral tonics
  • Topical oils, serums and shampoos (2–10% extract concentration)

Regulatory Status

Eclipta alba is recognized across major regulatory frameworks and is included in WHO monographs on medicinal plants.

  • FSSAI (India): permitted in traditional and nutraceutical categories
  • US FDA: used within dietary-supplement and topical cosmetic applications
  • EFSA (EU): permitted in food supplements; quality control based on phytochemical standardization

Sustainability & Sourcing

Cultivated in organic farms across India and Southeast Asia. A fast-growing herb that can be harvested 3–4 times annually, it ensures regenerative and sustainable supply chains. Ethically wildcrafted varieties are also available through certified partners.

Safety & Compliance

Safe for oral and topical use at recommended dosages, with no known toxicity in traditional doses. Extracts undergo stringent quality control to ensure heavy-metal, pesticide and microbial compliance, and are suitable for long-term use under medical supervision.
