Garcinia cambogia (Garcinia cambogia Desr.)

Known in Ayurveda as Vrikshamla or Malabar Tamarind, a tropical fruit-bearing tree whose pumpkin-shaped fruit rind has gained global attention as a natural aid for weight management, appetite control and metabolic balance — standardized today as a hydroxycitric-acid-rich botanical extract.

What is Vrikshamla?

Garcinia cambogia Desr., known across the Ayurvedic tradition as Vrikshamla or Malabar Tamarind, is a medium-sized evergreen tree of the family Guttiferae (Clusiaceae). It produces small, pumpkin-shaped yellow-to-green fruits whose rind is the primary part used for both medicinal and culinary purposes. When dried, the rind becomes dark brown and is commonly used in curries, pickles and traditional digestive preparations in Kerala and coastal Karnataka. In Ayurveda, Vrikshamla is considered Amla (sour) and Laghu (light), and holds a place in classical texts as a digestive, carminative and fat-reducing botanical.

Where Does It Grow?

Vrikshamla is a tree of warm, humid tropics. It thrives in Southern India — particularly Kerala and coastal Tamil Nadu — and across Southeast Asian plantations cultivated for export as a nutraceutical ingredient. The tree prefers shaded environments and is often found in evergreen forests and homestead gardens.

Geographical Distribution

  • Indonesia

Ideal Growing Conditions

The species is suited to the wet, evergreen forest belt of the tropics:

  • Climate: Warm, humid tropical conditions
  • Altitude: Low to mid elevations of the evergreen forest belt
  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soils in shaded settings
  • Rainfall: High, consistent rainfall typical of evergreen forest zones

Parts Used and Phytochemical Constituents

The fruit rind (pericarp) is the primary medicinal and culinary part. Its activity is driven by a distinctive profile of organic acids and polyphenols.

Key active compounds

  • Hydroxycitric acid (HCA) — the primary active compound, present at up to 30% in the dried rind
  • Citric acid and other organic acids
  • Polyphenols and flavonoids
  • Garcinol — anti-inflammatory and antioxidant

HCA works by inhibiting ATP citrate lyase, an enzyme involved in converting excess carbohydrates into fat, thereby reducing lipogenesis and potentially increasing satiety.

Therapeutic benefits

Garcinia offers a range of benefits focused on appetite regulation, fat metabolism, digestive function and anti-inflammatory support. It is especially suited to modern health challenges such as obesity, metabolic syndrome and overeating.

  • Suppresses lipogenesis — the conversion of carbohydrates into fat
  • Enhances fat oxidation and reduces fat accumulation in adipose tissue
  • Reduces appetite by increasing serotonin levels in the brain
  • Supports reduction of belly fat and waist circumference
  • Helps manage emotional eating and food cravings
  • Enhances satiety signals, reducing overall calorie intake
  • Stimulates digestive enzymes and improves gastric emptying
  • May reduce serum triglycerides, total cholesterol and LDL levels
  • Improves glucose tolerance and supports blood-sugar regulation in insulin-resistant individuals
  • Garcinol and polyphenols protect tissues from oxidative stress

Key research highlights

Vrikshamla has been extensively studied for its anti-obesity effects, with promising results in preclinical and clinical settings.

  • Weight loss: Randomized controlled trials have demonstrated modest weight loss and reduced BMI with Garcinia extract (HCA 1500–2800 mg/day)
  • Appetite suppression: Increased serotonin levels observed in animal models, correlating with decreased food intake
  • Lipid lowering: HCA supplementation reduced serum triglyceride and LDL levels in overweight subjects
  • Anti-inflammatory: Garcinol has shown COX-2 inhibition and antioxidant properties in laboratory studies

Results vary depending on dosage, formulation and lifestyle factors, emphasizing the importance of holistic integration alongside a balanced diet and physical activity.

Forms available

Vrikshamla is available in both traditional Ayurvedic and modern supplement forms. Its dried rind is used in cooking, while standardized extracts are used in metabolic-support supplements.

  • Dried rind (whole or powdered) — used in culinary and herbal formulations
  • Extracts standardized to 50–60% HCA, widely available in capsule form
  • Tablets, teas and syrups — often combined with Triphala or Guggulu
  • Culinary use as a tamarind substitute in coastal Indian dishes

Precautions and contraindications

While Vrikshamla is generally well tolerated, certain precautions should be observed, particularly with concentrated extracts:

  • Pregnancy and lactation: avoid due to insufficient safety data
  • Diabetes: may lower blood sugar — monitor if on antidiabetic drugs
  • Depression or anxiety: HCA increases serotonin levels and may interact with SSRIs or mood stabilizers
  • Liver disorders: use only under supervision, especially with high-concentration extracts; rare reports of liver stress exist in individuals taking high doses alongside other hepatotoxic agents
