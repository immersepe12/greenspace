Bhringaraja (Eclipta alba)

The "King of Hair" — a multipurpose Ayurvedic rejuvenator prized for promoting hair growth, reversing premature graying, and supporting the liver, skin and overall vitality.

What is Bhringaraja?

Bhringaraja is a fast-growing, creeping herbaceous plant recognized for its small, daisy-like white flowers. Botanically named Eclipta alba (Linn.) Hassk., it belongs to the Asteraceae family. The name "Bhringaraja" in Sanskrit literally means "King of Hair," highlighting its most famous traditional use in promoting hair growth and reversing premature graying.

In Ayurveda, Bhringaraja is categorized as a Rasayana (rejuvenative herb) and is known for its affinity toward Pitta and Kapha doshas. It is considered cooling, bitter, and slightly pungent in taste, and it works on the liver, skin, and scalp with notable detoxifying and revitalizing effects. Beyond its reputation as a natural remedy for scalp and hair, Bhringaraja is valued across Ayurveda, Siddha and Unani as a multipurpose rejuvenator for the liver, skin and overall health.

Where Does It Grow?

Eclipta alba is a cosmopolitan herb found in tropical and subtropical regions across the globe. This plant prefers moist soil and grows abundantly in marshy areas, riverbanks, paddy fields and forest clearings. Due to its resilience and adaptability, Bhringaraja is also cultivated as a medicinal herb in home gardens and herbal farms.

Parts Used & Phytochemical Composition

The whole plant is medicinal: roots, stems, leaves and flowers are all used for therapeutic purposes. Among these, the leaves are the most potent and commonly harvested part. The key phytochemicals include:

  • Wedelolactone — the principal hepatoprotective coumestan
  • Ecliptine
  • Luteolin
  • β-amyrin
  • Flavonoids
  • Coumestans
  • Alkaloids and triterpenes

Wedelolactone, in particular, has been extensively studied for its hepatoprotective and anti-inflammatory action, while the flavonoids and coumestans contribute strong antioxidant and radical-scavenging activity.

Traditional & Therapeutic Uses

Bhringaraja is used both internally and externally across a variety of traditional medicine systems. Its diverse applications range from liver disorders to cosmetic hair treatments.

Hair & scalp

Traditionally, Bhringaraja is believed to strengthen hair roots, promote hair growth and help reverse premature graying — earning it the title "King of Hair."

Liver & detoxification

Bhringaraja is a classic liver detox herb in Ayurveda. It helps in conditions like jaundice, fatty liver and hepatitis, supporting the body's natural detoxification pathways.

Skin & immunity

Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial action helps soothe and protect the skin, while its antioxidant content boosts immune function.

Digestion

Its bitter principles stimulate digestive enzymes and may help with sluggish digestion and overall gut health.

How to Use Bhringaraja

Bhringaraja is available in multiple forms for both internal and external use. The ideal preparation depends on the purpose.

  • Fresh Juice (Swarasa) — extracted from leaves; potent and fast-acting
  • Powder (Churna) — dried and ground form of the whole plant
  • Oil (Taila) — infused in coconut or sesame oil, primarily for hair and skin
  • Capsules / Tablets — standardized extracts for internal use
  • Tea / Infusion — leaves boiled in water and consumed

Always consult a qualified herbalist for personalized dosage and use. Store powder and oil in cool, dry places away from sunlight, and use within the recommended period for best efficacy.

Evidence-Based Applications

Modern research supports many of Bhringaraja's traditional uses:

  • Liver health — Wedelolactone, a key compound in Bhringaraja, has shown hepatoprotective effects in animal studies.
  • Hair growth — Studies demonstrate enhanced hair follicle activity and reduced hair loss with topical application of Bhringaraja extract.
  • Anti-inflammatory — Flavonoids and coumestans exhibit strong anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Precautions & Contraindications

Bhringaraja is generally considered safe for most adults when used appropriately. However, proper preparation, dosage and guidance are important. Some considerations include:

  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding — limited safety data; avoid unless prescribed
  • Low blood pressure — use with caution due to its hypotensive effect
  • Patients on liver medications — may interact with hepatoprotective or hepatotoxic drugs
  • Allergic individuals — always do a patch test before using topically
